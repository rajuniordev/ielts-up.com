Exercise 3: relevant answers in IELTS Speaking

This exercise will help you to get a better understanding of how to give a relevant answer in IELTS Speaking test. To complete the exercise, listen to each of the audio recordings below and think of what the question asks you to do.

Before doing this exercise, you can check out IELTS-up lesson on how to give relevant answers in IELTS Speaking test.

We have done an example for you:


Example: what does this question ask you to do?

Do you think that the city where you live is a dangerous place?
   give a reason
   give an opinion
   compare or contrast
   suggest

This question starts with a phase 'do you think', indicating that you're expected to give your opinion. But to answer this question properly, you should give an opinion and also support it with a reason. It is not obvious, but giving reason for your opinion is always a good practice.


Try out these questions:

(you can give more than 1 answer)

1. This question asks you to ...

What does your job involve?
   describe
   explain
   give a preference
   suggest


2. To give a relevant answer, you have to ...

What kinds of films do you like the best?
   compare or contrast
   give a reason
   give a preference
   suggest


3. What does this question ask you to do?

Why do you like your work?
   describe
   give a preference
   give a reason
   suggest


4. This question asks you to ...

Do you like to study alone or with your friends?
   describe
   give a preference
   give a reason
   suggest


5. In this question you have to ...

What places should a tourist visit in your homecountry?
   describe
   give a reason
   compare or contrast
   suggest


6. This question asks you to ...

What are some differences between this city and your hometown?
   give a preference
   give an opinion
   compare or contrast
   suggest


7. This question asks you to ...

How did you learn English in your country?
   give a preference
   give an opinion
   give a preference
   describe
